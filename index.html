Amok Trey (Fish Amok)
Cambodia's national dish. Flaky fish smothered in kroeung paste and coconut milk, traditionally steamed in a banana leaf bowl.
Nom Banh Chok (Khmer Noodles)
Often eaten for breakfast. Fermented rice noodles covered in a rich, lemongrass-based fish gravy, topped with fresh cucumber, mint, and banana blossom.
Bai Sach Chrouk
A beloved morning staple featuring thinly sliced pork marinated in garlic and soy sauce, served over broken rice with a clear broth and pickled vegetables.
Kuy Teav
A popular rice noodle soup usually made with a rich pork or beef broth, bean sprouts, and fresh herbs.
Beef Lok Lak
Tender stir-fried cubes of beef served on a bed of lettuce, cucumber, and tomatoes, typically dipped in a sauce of lime juice and Kampot pepper.
Samlar Korkor
A highly nutritious and hearty traditional soup packed with seasonal vegetables, herbs, and usually catfish or pork, flavored with roasted rice and fermented fish paste (prahok)

Meet Our Professional Chefs

Discover the visionary culinary artists preserving heritage and shaping the future of Cambodian cuisine.

Chef Nak’s Culinary World
Where Tradition Meets Innovation

Chef Nak (Rotanak Ros) is an award-winning Cambodian chef, author, and culinary ambassador preserving and elevating Khmer cuisine through research, storytelling, and unforgettable hospitality. Based in Phnom Penh and Siem Reap, she leads a modern Cambodian culinary movement rooted in heritage, bringing royal and home recipes back into everyday conversation and onto the world stage.

Sao Sopheak
Cambodia’s Culinary Alchemist

In 2019, he won the bronze medal in the “Global Chef” category at the Thailand Ultimate Chef Challenge. His cookbook, which in 2022 won the Gourmet World Cookbook Award for best Asian cookbook, further enhanced his reputation. In 2024, he was named Best Master Chef at the Best Awards in Singapore, and in 2025 he became the first ambassador for Sturia caviar in Cambodia.
